    Cellular Automata

    A cellular automaton (automata is the plural of automaton) is a grid of cells, each of which is in one of several possible discrete states. For example a cell can be either 1 or 0/on or off/red, green or blue/etc. An initial state is applied to each cell in the automaton and this grid will be known as generation 0. Further generations are generated by applying a predetermined ruleset to each of the cells in the grid which may cause a change in state. Generally a ruleset takes into account the cell the ruleset is being applied to and a neighbourhood of cells around that cell. For example, in a 2D automaton, a Von Neuman Neighbourhood ruleset has the center (target cell), north, south, east abd west cells. A Moore neighbourhood consists of the target cell and all adjacent cells.

    Elementary Cellular Automata

    An elementary cellular automaton is a one dimensional cellular automaton where each cell can only be 1 or 0 and the next generation is determined by a ruleset which is only applied to the target cell and its two immediate neighbours. If we take the automaton 0010 and target the cell that has a value of 1 then the neighbourhood we apply the ruleset to is 010.
